Conclusion

What have been done?

On the basis of BDI and Kripke models specified in 'Problem Description' section a model has been built. This model consists of two main parts:

  • Civilization model. This part describes a model used to represent a civilization. Each civilization consists of a number of basic parameters:
    • Name. A civilization chosen name.
    • Attitude. A general outlook towards other societies in space. Malevolent civilizations generally strive to destroy civilizations they encounter, while benevolent ones are much more likely to try and make contact with alien society.
    • Technical level. A real number in range 0-10. This number represents technical prowess of the population. It being a real number constitutes subtle difference even between civilizations of the same scale.
    • Depth of suspicion. An integer number in range 0-7. Represents societal arguments about other civilization's attitude. Is it worth making contact, if another society may be malevolent? Or, maybe, if another society is benevolent, but thinks that we are malevolent and may try to destroy us anyway?
    • State automaton. A representation of BDI model. Possible actions to take at any given point in time. Slightly different for different types of civilizations. For example, a malevolent society does not have an opportunity to make contact, while benevolent civilization does not consider a possibility of outright extermination of another society.
  • Statistics gathering engine. This engine is based on running a number of encounters between two civilizations. By the end of the encounter each of two civilizations either defended itself by destroying other civilization and survived alone, prevailed and destroyed the other one (hence also surviving alone), hid and thus removed a possibility of destruction or cooperated with other one. We provide a command-line based text interface that allows user to:
    • Make a manual run of one particular encounter simulation.
    • Make an interactive run of one particular encounter assuming a role of one of the societies participating in an encounter
    • Make a user-specified number of runs and gather a statistics
